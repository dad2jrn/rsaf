# Template — Discovery Requests Targeting Chain of Title and Ownership

> **Usage:** These requests must be adapted to the forum, local rules, and specific facts of each matter. Virginia General District Court does not provide for formal discovery in the same manner as Circuit Court. In GDC, information may be gathered through the pre-trial exchange process or at the hearing. In Circuit Court, formal interrogatories and requests for production may be served under the Virginia Rules of Supreme Court. In AAA arbitration, document requests are governed by the AAA Consumer Arbitration Rules. Verify the applicable procedures before serving any discovery.
>
> All requests marked [BANK OF AMERICA] or [CAPITAL ONE] are tailored to those programs. Remove inapplicable requests and adapt as needed.

---

## Part 1 — Requests for Production

### A. Chain of Title — All Cases

**Request 1.** Produce all documents — including executed bills of sale, assignments, reassignments, transfer agreements, and all schedules, exhibits, and attachments — on which Plaintiff relies to establish its present right to enforce the receivable sued upon.

**Request 2.** Produce the complete chain of title for the receivable from the originating creditor to Plaintiff, including every intermediate transfer document.

**Request 3.** Produce any account schedule, collateral schedule, exhibit, attachment, or supplement to any bill of sale or assignment that identifies Defendant's account by account number.

**Request 4.** Produce any document showing that the receivable was never transferred from the originating bank. If Plaintiff asserts a retention theory (i.e., the originating bank never transferred the receivable), produce all documents supporting that contention.

**Request 5.** Produce any document showing that the receivable was transferred into a securitization trust and subsequently removed, including: the trust's removal notice, removal schedule, or any document reflecting the trust's transfer of the receivable back to the originating bank or any intermediate entity.

**Request 6.** Produce the executed reassignment instrument by which any trust transferred the receivable back to the originating bank or any other entity before the receivable reached Plaintiff.

---

### B. Securitization Program Documents

**Request 7.** Produce the Receivables Purchase Agreement (or equivalent) under which the originating bank transferred the receivable to any bankruptcy-remote entity or depositor.

**Request 8.** Produce the Pooling and Servicing Agreement, Transfer and Servicing Agreement, or equivalent governing agreement under which any depositor or transferor conveyed the receivable to any issuing trust.

**Request 9.** Produce the trust agreement, owner trust agreement, or indenture for any trust in which Plaintiff contends the receivable was held.

**Request 10.** Produce any account schedule, collateral certificate, or supplement that identifies Defendant's account as included in any trust.

### C. Bank of America Specific [BANK OF AMERICA]

**Request 11.** [BofA] Produce all documents identifying whether the account was originated by Bank of America, N.A., FIA Card Services, N.A., or MBNA America Bank, N.A., and documents establishing the chain of corporate succession for that entity.

**Request 12.** [BofA] Produce the Receivables Purchase Agreement between [Bank of America, N.A. / FIA Card Services, N.A.] and BA Credit Card Funding, LLC, including all amendments and restatements, and any account schedule identifying Defendant's account.

**Request 13.** [BofA] Produce the Pooling and Servicing Agreement for BA Master Credit Card Trust II, including all amendments and restatements, and any collateral certificate or account schedule identifying Defendant's receivable as included in the trust.

**Request 14.** [BofA] Produce the removal notice, removal schedule, or trustee documentation reflecting the removal of Defendant's receivable from BA Master Credit Card Trust II.

**Request 15.** [BofA] Produce the reassignment instrument by which BA Master Credit Card Trust II reassigned Defendant's receivable to BA Credit Card Funding, LLC or Bank of America, N.A.

### D. Capital One Specific [CAPITAL ONE]

**Request 16.** [Capital One] Identify and produce all documents establishing whether the account was originated by Capital One Bank (USA), N.A. or Capital One, N.A., and produce all documents supporting that identification.

**Request 17.** [Capital One] Produce the Receivables Purchase Agreement between [Capital One Bank (USA), N.A. / Capital One, N.A.] and Capital One Funding, LLC, including all amendments and restatements, and any account schedule identifying Defendant's account.

**Request 18.** [Capital One] Produce the Amended and Restated Pooling and Servicing Agreement (or equivalent) governing Capital One Master Trust (COMT), including all amendments and restatements, and any account schedule or collateral certificate identifying Defendant's receivable as included in COMT.

**Request 19.** [Capital One] Produce the removal notice, removal schedule, or other documentation reflecting the removal of Defendant's receivable from Capital One Master Trust under PSA § 6.01 (Ineligible Receivables) or § 6.02 (Breach of Representations) or any other provision.

**Request 20.** [Capital One] Produce the reassignment instrument by which Capital One Master Trust reassigned Defendant's receivable to Capital One Funding, LLC or Capital One Bank (USA), N.A.

### E. Servicing and Account Records

**Request 21.** Produce the complete payment and transaction history for Defendant's account from account opening through charge-off or last activity.

**Request 22.** Produce the cardmember agreement governing Defendant's account, including the version in effect at the time the account was opened and any subsequent version in effect at the time of default or charge-off.

**Request 23.** Produce the arbitration agreement or arbitration clause governing Defendant's account, if any.

**Request 24.** Produce all monthly account statements for Defendant's account.

**Request 25.** Produce the charge-off records and charge-off authorization documents for Defendant's account, including any internal policy or record establishing the date and basis for the charge-off.

---

### F. Authentication and Foundation

**Request 26.** Produce all affidavits, certifications, or declarations that Plaintiff intends to offer in evidence, along with any documents authenticated by or described in those affidavits.

**Request 27.** Produce all documents reflecting the records-management policies and procedures under which the account records and chain-of-title documents were created and maintained by the originating bank, any intermediate entity, and Plaintiff.

**Request 28.** Identify and produce the source documents for every fact asserted in Plaintiff's affidavit(s) or declaration(s), including the source of the claimed balance, the source of the claimed charge-off date, and the source of any claim about chain of title.

## Part 2 — Interrogatories

**Interrogatory 1.** Identify every entity — by full legal name and entity type — that has owned, held, serviced, transferred, or otherwise had an interest in the receivable sued upon, from origination through the date of this interrogatory, in chronological order.

**Interrogatory 2.** State whether Plaintiff contends that the receivable was ever held in a securitization trust. If yes, identify the trust by name, the governing agreement, the date the receivable entered the trust, and the date and method by which the receivable left the trust.

**Interrogatory 3.** If Plaintiff contends the receivable was never transferred into a securitization trust, state all facts supporting that contention and identify all documents on which Plaintiff relies.

**Interrogatory 4.** If Plaintiff contends the receivable was transferred into a securitization trust and subsequently removed, state: (a) the provision of the governing agreement under which the removal occurred; (b) the date of removal; (c) the entity to which the receivable was reassigned; and (d) every document evidencing the removal and reassignment.

**Interrogatory 5.** Identify the full legal name of the entity that sold or transferred the receivable directly to Plaintiff, and state the date and terms of that transfer.

**Interrogatory 6.** Identify by name, title, employer, and contact information every individual who has knowledge of the chain of title for the receivable, and describe the nature of each individual's knowledge.

**Interrogatory 7.** State the basis for the balance claimed in Plaintiff's [Complaint / Warrant in Debt], including: (a) the principal amount at charge-off; (b) any post-charge-off interest or fees added by Plaintiff or any prior holder; (c) the applicable contractual interest rate; and (d) the date through which interest has been calculated.

**Interrogatory 8.** [Capital One only] State whether the originating account was originated by Capital One Bank (USA), N.A. or by Capital One, N.A., and state the basis for Plaintiff's answer.

**Interrogatory 9.** [Bank of America only] State whether the originating account was originated by Bank of America, N.A., FIA Card Services, N.A., or MBNA America Bank, N.A., and state the basis for Plaintiff's answer, including the identity of any corporate succession documents (merger certificates, name-change documents, or FDIC bank data records) on which Plaintiff relies to establish the relationship between the originating entity and the current plaintiff or servicer.

## Part 3 — Requests for Admission

**Admission 1.** Admit that monthly account statements sent to the account holder do not, by themselves, constitute an executed assignment of the receivable.

**Admission 2.** Admit that Plaintiff bears the burden of proving its present legal right to enforce the receivable.

**Admission 3.** Admit that the receivable was at any time held in a securitization trust. [Or, if asserting the contrary:] Admit that the receivable was never held in a securitization trust.

**Admission 4.** Admit that Plaintiff has not produced an executed account schedule specifically identifying Defendant's account as included in any transfer from the originating bank to any intermediate entity.

**Admission 5.** Admit that Plaintiff has not produced an executed removal notice or removal schedule specifically identifying Defendant's receivable as having been removed from any securitization trust.

**Admission 6.** Admit that Plaintiff has not produced an executed reassignment instrument specifically identifying Defendant's account or receivable as having been transferred from any trust to any entity in the chain leading to Plaintiff.

**Admission 7.** Admit that the bill of sale, assignment, or other instrument on which Plaintiff relies to prove ownership does not itself identify Defendant's account by account number. [Adapt based on what Plaintiff has produced.]

> **Adaptation notes:**
>
> - Remove requests that are not applicable to the specific bank or theory.
> - Add requests based on the specific documents Plaintiff has and has not produced.
> - Requests for Admission should be calibrated to admissions that are demonstrably true on the record — do not seek admissions as to contested facts in a way that invites tactical denials.
> - Virginia Circuit Court: interrogatories and RFP are subject to numerical limits under local rules; verify limits before serving.
> - AAA arbitration: document requests are governed by AAA Consumer Arbitration Rule 22; cooperate with the arbitrator's scheduling of document exchange.
