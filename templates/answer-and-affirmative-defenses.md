## Affirmative Defenses

### First Affirmative Defense — Failure to Establish Standing / Present Right to Enforce

Plaintiff bears the burden of proving its present legal right to enforce the receivable sued upon. To the extent Plaintiff relies on ownership of the receivable, Plaintiff must establish that ownership through competent, admissible, account-specific evidence.

### Second Affirmative Defense — Failure of Proof of Chain of Title

To the extent the receivable was assigned, transferred, securitized, removed, or reacquired, Plaintiff must prove an unbroken chain of title sufficient to establish its present right to enforce the specific receivable at issue.

### Third Affirmative Defense — Failure to State a Claim

Plaintiff's pleading fails to allege facts sufficient to establish each required element of its claim, including Plaintiff's present right to enforce the receivable.

### Reservation

Defendant reserves the right to amend these defenses as permitted by the applicable rules after discovery or production of additional documents.
