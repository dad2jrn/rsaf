# Template — Defendant's Answer and Affirmative Defenses

> **Usage:** This template must be adapted to the specific court, local rules, procedural posture, facts, and applicable law of each matter. Every bracketed item must be completed. Every defense must be reviewed against the facts; remove any defense without a factual and legal basis. RSAF does not draft court-facing documents without a specific request; this template is a framework for adaptation, not a ready-to-file document.
>
> **Virginia GDC note:** In General District Court, the filing format is simplified. A written answer filed with the clerk before the return date, or an oral response at the hearing, may suffice depending on local practice. Verify local GDC procedure before filing.
>
> **Virginia Circuit Court note:** A responsive pleading in Circuit Court is governed by the Virginia Rules of Supreme Court. A Plea in Bar (for certain threshold defenses), Demurrer (for failure to state a claim), and Answer may be filed separately or combined depending on timing and local practice.

## Affirmative Defenses

Defendant hereby asserts the following affirmative defenses. Defendant does not assume any burden by pleading these defenses. Plaintiff retains the burden of proving each element of its claims. Assertion of these defenses is not an admission of any allegation.

---

### First Affirmative Defense — Lack of Standing; Plaintiff Has Not Established Its Present Right to Enforce

Plaintiff bears the burden of proving its present legal right to enforce the receivable it seeks to collect. Filing a lawsuit does not establish that right.

To enforce a debt through legal process, Plaintiff must prove that it presently holds the legal right to collect this specific receivable from this specific Defendant. Where the receivable has been originated by a national bank, securitized through a trust structure, and subsequently charged off, removed, and reassigned, proof of that right requires: (a) an unbroken chain of documented transfers from the originating bank to the Plaintiff, (b) with account-specific documentation at each step that identifies Defendant's account or receivable, (c) authenticated by a witness competent to lay the business records foundation for each entity in the chain.

Plaintiff has [not produced / produced insufficient] evidence establishing its present right to enforce. Accordingly, Plaintiff has not satisfied this element of its claim.

---

### Second Affirmative Defense — Failure of Proof on the Ownership Element

Plaintiff has not produced competent, admissible, account-specific evidence establishing that it presently owns the right to enforce the receivable. This is a required element of each claim asserted.

Program-level documents — such as pooling and servicing agreements, prospectuses, trust agreements, and similar instruments — may establish how a securitization program is structured. They do not prove what happened to Defendant's specific receivable. Plaintiff must produce account-level documentation specifically identifying Defendant's account at each stage of any claimed transfer.

To the extent Plaintiff has not produced such documentation, it has not carried its burden on the ownership element, and Plaintiff cannot prevail regardless of what Plaintiff's servicing records show about account balances, payment history, or charge-off status.

---

### Third Affirmative Defense — Failure to State a Claim / Demurrer

[Use where applicable to the forum.] Plaintiff's [Complaint / Warrant in Debt] fails to allege facts sufficient to establish each required element of the claim asserted, including Plaintiff's present right to enforce the receivable. Plaintiff's pleading does not identify the chain of title from the originating creditor to Plaintiff, does not identify the transfer documents on which Plaintiff relies, and does not allege account-specific facts supporting its claimed ownership.

### Fourth Affirmative Defense — Statute of Limitations

[Use where applicable.] Plaintiff's claim is barred by the applicable statute of limitations. Under Va. Code § 8.01-246(2), an action on a written contract signed by the defendant must be brought within five years. Under Va. Code § 8.01-246(4), an action on other contracts must be brought within three years.

[Identify administrator if known, e.g., American Arbitration Association Consumer Arbitration Rules.] The Federal Arbitration Act, 9 U.S.C. § 2, governs the enforceability of this provision. Defendant invokes the arbitration provision and requests that this action be stayed or dismissed pending arbitration.

[Note: Confirm the arbitration clause exists and applies to this dispute before asserting this defense. Obtain and review the applicable cardmember agreement vintage. Arbitration clauses vary over time.]

### Sixth Affirmative Defense — Lack of Privity / Plaintiff Is Not the Real Party in Interest

[Use where Plaintiff is a debt buyer and chain of title is contested.] Plaintiff is not the original creditor on this account. To the extent Plaintiff has not established by competent evidence that it acquired the right to enforce the receivable through a documented chain of valid transfers, Plaintiff is not the real party in interest and may not maintain this action.

---

### Seventh Affirmative Defense — Failure to Satisfy Business Records Foundation

[Use where Plaintiff's evidence appears to rely on records for which foundation has not been established.] Plaintiff relies on account records, statements, and affidavits that constitute hearsay. Admission of this evidence requires satisfaction of the business records exception under Va. Rules Evid. Rule 2:803(6). To the extent any document Plaintiff offers: was created by an entity other than Plaintiff; is authenticated by a custodian who was not employed by the originating entity; does not reflect knowledge at the time of the event recorded; or bears indicia of untrustworthiness — Defendant reserves the right to challenge the admissibility of such evidence.

Defendant does not waive any defense not expressly asserted herein to the extent permitted by applicable procedural rules.
