# Template — Motion to Compel Arbitration and Stay or Dismiss Litigation

> **Usage:** Use only where the cardmember agreement contains a binding arbitration clause that covers this dispute and has not been waived by Defendant's litigation conduct. Before filing, verify: (1) the clause exists and is quoted accurately; (2) the clause covers the type of dispute at issue; (3) Defendant has not previously invoked court jurisdiction in a way that constitutes waiver; (4) the plaintiff has not itself waived arbitration; (5) the applicable AAA or other administrator rules.
>
> The Federal Arbitration Act, 9 U.S.C. §§ 1 et seq., governs the enforceability of arbitration agreements in consumer credit-card matters where the card agreement involves interstate commerce (which is virtually always). AT&T Mobility LLC v. Concepcion, 563 U.S. 333 (2011) confirms that class-action waivers in consumer arbitration clauses are enforceable under the FAA. Verify current governing authority before citing.
>
> Virginia state courts must enforce valid arbitration agreements under the FAA; Virginia Code § 8.01-581.01 et seq. also applies. Verify applicable procedure in the specific forum.

## Argument

### I. The Federal Arbitration Act Requires Enforcement of the Arbitration Agreement

The Federal Arbitration Act, 9 U.S.C. § 2, provides that written arbitration agreements "shall be valid, irrevocable, and enforceable, save upon such grounds as exist at law or in equity for the revocation of any contract." The FAA "establishes a national policy favoring arbitration." AT&T Mobility LLC v. Concepcion, 563 U.S. 333, 339 (2011).

A court presented with a motion to compel arbitration must: (1) determine whether there is a valid agreement to arbitrate; and (2) determine whether the dispute falls within the scope of that agreement. Both questions are answered in the affirmative here.

### II. A Valid Arbitration Agreement Exists

The cardmember agreement, attached as Exhibit A, is a valid written contract. The arbitration clause within it is supported by the same consideration as the agreement as a whole. [Add any argument specific to the formation of this agreement if anticipated challenges exist, e.g., whether Defendant assented to the terms by using the card.]

### III. This Dispute Falls Within the Scope of the Arbitration Clause

The arbitration clause covers [describe scope — e.g., "any claim or dispute arising out of or relating to the account, the cardmember agreement, or any transaction on the account"]. Plaintiff's [breach of contract / account stated / other] claim arises directly from the account and falls squarely within this scope.

### IV. Defendant Has Not Waived Arbitration

[Defendant raises arbitration at the first available opportunity. / Defendant has not filed counterclaims, sought discovery inconsistent with arbitration, or otherwise invoked the jurisdiction of this Court in a manner inconsistent with the right to arbitrate.]

### V. This Court Should Stay [or Dismiss] This Action

9 U.S.C. § 3 requires a court to stay an action pending arbitration when a valid arbitration clause covers the dispute. [Some courts dismiss rather than stay when all claims are subject to arbitration; state what relief is requested and why.] This Court should stay [or dismiss] the action and direct the parties to proceed to arbitration.

> **Checklist before filing:**
>
> - [ ] Cardmember agreement obtained and arbitration clause located and read in full.
> - [ ] Arbitration clause is quoted verbatim in the motion — not paraphrased.
> - [ ] Claims fall within the scope of the clause as written.
> - [ ] Defendant has not previously waived arbitration by filing a counterclaim, pursuing court discovery, or taking other steps inconsistent with arbitration.
> - [ ] The cardmember agreement is attached as an exhibit and properly authenticated (or will be at the hearing).
> - [ ] AT&T Mobility v. Concepcion and any other cases cited have been verified for current validity.
> - [ ] AAA filing fee provisions checked — in AAA consumer arbitration, the business typically pays most fees; confirm with current AAA Consumer Arbitration Rules.
> - [ ] Motion filed before answer due date or simultaneously with answer, consistent with local procedure.
>
> **Capital One note:** Capital One cardmember agreements have historically required arbitration under AAA Consumer Arbitration Rules. Capital One routinely files AAA demands against cardholders and has an established arbitration practice. If Plaintiff has already initiated AAA arbitration, the motion to compel arbitration is moot; respond under AAA rules instead.
>
> **Bank of America note:** Bank of America (formerly FIA Card Services) cardmember agreements have also historically included arbitration clauses. Confirm which version of the cardmember agreement governs the specific account vintage, as arbitration clause terms have changed over time.
