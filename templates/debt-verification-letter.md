# Template — Debt Verification / Proof of Assignment Request

> **Usage Note — Read Before Sending**
>
> **Statutory basis.** The right to request debt validation from a debt collector arises under 15 U.S.C. § 1692g (FDCPA). A debt collector that receives a timely validation dispute must cease collection activity until it provides verification of the debt. The dispute must be sent within 30 days of the debt collector's initial communication to the consumer. After that 30-day window, the obligation to cease collection lapses, though the underlying right to dispute the debt's validity survives.
>
> **Threshold question — is the plaintiff a "debt collector"?** FDCPA validation rights apply only when the plaintiff qualifies as a "debt collector" under 15 U.S.C. § 1692a(6). Original creditors suing on debts they originated and retained are not debt collectors and have no FDCPA validation obligation. Debt buyers present a more complex question: under *Henson v. Santander Consumer USA Inc.*, 582 U.S. 79 (2017), a party that purchases debts for its own account and collects them may not qualify as a debt collector because it is collecting debts owed to itself, not debts owed to another. See Chapter 15 (Federal Law) for analysis of when a plaintiff is or is not a debt collector under the FDCPA.
>
> **Two distinct tools.** This template serves two legally distinct purposes that should not be confused:
> 1. *FDCPA pre-suit validation right (§1692g):* A statutory right that exists before litigation. If the plaintiff is a debt collector and the 30-day window is open, a validation dispute triggers the obligation to cease collection and provide verification. A failure to verify is independently actionable under the FDCPA.
> 2. *Trial burden of proof:* The plaintiff's obligation to prove present legal right to enforce exists at trial regardless of whether the FDCPA applies. This letter also documents that the defendant disputes ownership and demands proof — which is relevant to the litigation burden of proof even if no FDCPA right exists.
>
> Identify which basis applies before sending and adapt the letter accordingly. If FDCPA rights do not apply (original creditor; debt buyer not qualifying as debt collector), the letter still creates a written record of dispute but carries no statutory ceasing-collection obligation.
>
> **Cross-reference:** Chapter 15 (Federal Law — FDCPA and FAA).

```text
[DATE]

[COLLECTOR / PLAINTIFF / COUNSEL]
[ADDRESS]

Re: Request for Validation and Proof of Present Right to Enforce
Account: [ACCOUNT IDENTIFIER]
Case No.: [CASE NUMBER, if applicable]

To Whom It May Concern:

I dispute the alleged debt and request verification of the debt and proof of your present legal right to collect or enforce it.

Please provide all documents establishing your authority to collect this specific receivable, including but not limited to:

1. The cardmember agreement allegedly governing the account.
2. The complete chain of title for the receivable.
3. Any bill of sale, assignment, reassignment, or transfer agreement identifying the account.
4. Any schedule, exhibit, or account-level record linking the alleged receivable to me.
5. Any document showing that the receivable was never transferred, if that is your position.
6. Any document showing that the receivable was transferred and later reacquired, if that is your position.
7. The name of the current creditor or owner of the receivable.

This request is not an admission of liability. All rights are reserved.

Sincerely,

[NAME]
```
