# 12 — Citation Integrity

## 12.1 Core Rule

No citation may be used unless it is real, verified, and accurately characterized.

## 12.2 Required Citation Metadata

For each case, RSAF should know:

- Case name.
- Reporter citation or reliable database citation.
- Court.
- Year.
- Holding.
- Binding or persuasive status.
- Exact proposition supported.
- Limitations or adverse treatment, if known.

## 12.3 No Fabricated Quotations

Do not quote from a case, statute, regulation, or filing unless the quotation has been verified against the source.

## 12.4 Green v. Portfolio Recovery Associates

**Citation:** Mazie Green v. Portfolio Recovery Associates, LLC, No. 0144-22-3, 80 Va.App. 119, 897 S.E.2d 275 (Va. Ct. App. 2024) (en banc)

**Procedural history.** PRA obtained judgment against Green in circuit court ($8,914.31). A three-judge panel of the Court of Appeals reversed in February 2024, finding PRA had not proven ownership through chain of title. The full court granted rehearing en banc. In December 2024, the en banc court reversed the panel and affirmed the circuit court's judgment for PRA.

**En banc holding.** The en banc court affirmed the circuit court's judgment in PRA's favor. It found that material facts about the alleged assignment were in dispute, and reasonable factfinders could conclude that PRA's documentation — four bills of sale, a Synchrony Bank affidavit, a data sheet, billing statements, and a custodian declaration — sufficiently established chain of title, notwithstanding account-number discrepancies in the documentary record.

**Critical framing issue — standing vs. merits.**

The en banc majority treated proof of ownership as a **merits and sufficiency-of-the-evidence** question, not a threshold standing question. A pure standing challenge was defeated at the summary-judgment stage on relatively thin evidence because the court found that factual disputes about ownership precluded summary judgment in Green's favor.

This has direct procedural consequences: framing a chain-of-title challenge solely as a "standing" challenge may fail at summary judgment under the *Morgan* / *Green* framework. The more durable frame is **failure to prove ownership as a merits element at trial** — i.e., the plaintiff did not carry its burden on the ownership element of the claim.

The issue should be preserved in both terms distinctly — as a standing argument and as a failure-of-proof-on-the-merits argument — so that neither framing is waived.

**Where the chain-of-title analysis actually lives.**

The thorough chain-of-title analysis in *Green* is found in Judge Causey's en banc dissent (joined by Chaney) and in the vacated panel opinion — both persuasive authority only. For binding Virginia authority on chain-of-title requirements, build from the underlying cases *Green* itself relied on: *Tennent's Heirs v. Pattons*, 33 Va. 196 (1835); *Lambert v. Barker*, 232 Va. 21 (1986); *Lewis's Ex'r v. Bacon's Legatee*, 13 Va. 89 (1808); *Union Recovery Ltd. P'ship v. Horton*, 252 Va. 418 (1996); and *Cemetery Consultants, Inc. v. Tidewater Funeral Dirs. Assocs.*, 219 Va. 1001 (1979). See Chapter 18 for verified citations to these and related authorities.

**What the case does and does not establish.**

*Green* confirms that Virginia courts examine chain-of-title evidence when ownership is contested, and that ownership is a required element the plaintiff must prove. It does not establish that bulk bills of sale without account-level schedules are sufficient proof — PRA's judgment was affirmed at the summary-judgment stage because factual disputes remained; the ultimate merits of PRA's evidence were not resolved by the en banc court.

The vacated panel opinion is not binding authority and should not be cited without disclosing its vacated status.

**Caution for RSAF use.** Before citing *Green* in any court-facing document, read the en banc opinion directly. Identify precisely which proposition you are citing it for and whether that proposition comes from the majority, a concurrence, or the dissent. Do not cite the dissent or the vacated panel opinion as binding authority.

## 12.5 Authority Classification

| Class | Description |
|---|---|
| A | Binding authority directly on point |
| B | Binding authority by analogy |
| C | Persuasive authority |
| D | Statutory or regulatory text requiring interpretation |
| E | Contractual/public document evidence |
| F | Analytical inference requiring express qualification |

RSAF should not present Class F reasoning as though it were Class A authority.
