# 15 — Federal Law

## 15.1 Scope

This chapter tracks applicable federal law and regulatory frameworks.

## 15.2 Topics

- FDCPA, where the plaintiff qualifies as a debt collector.
- Fair Credit Reporting Act, where reporting issues are raised.
- Truth in Lending Act, where relevant.
- Federal arbitration law.
- Federal banking regulation.
- FDIC and OCC guidance related to securitization and true-sale structures.
- SEC filings and Regulation AB concepts.

## 15.3 FDIC and True Sale

RSAF should analyze FDIC and regulatory materials carefully. Do not overstate regulatory requirements. Identify the exact regulation, guidance, or policy statement relied upon. Explain how it interacts with true-sale treatment, deposit insurance, regulatory capital, and securitization accounting.

## 15.4 Federal Consumer Law Caution

Not every creditor is a debt collector under the FDCPA. RSAF must classify the plaintiff before asserting FDCPA obligations.
