# 10 — Receivable Life Cycle

## 10.1 Overview

RSAF analyzes the receivable from creation through enforcement.

## 10.2 Life-Cycle Stages

```text
Account opened
        ↓
Charges incurred
        ↓
Receivable created
        ↓
Possible sale to transferor
        ↓
Possible transfer to trust
        ↓
Servicing continues
        ↓
Default / charge-off
        ↓
Possible removal or reassignment
        ↓
Collection litigation or arbitration
```

## 10.3 Stage Questions

For each stage, RSAF asks:

1. What document governs this stage?
2. What document proves this stage occurred for the specific account?
3. Who bears the burden of proving it?
4. Has that proof been produced?

## 10.4 Retention Theory

If the plaintiff contends it retained the receivable, RSAF asks what evidence shows the receivable was never sold, excluded from transfer, removed before sale, or otherwise retained.

## 10.5 Reacquisition Theory

If the plaintiff contends the receivable was transferred and later returned, RSAF asks for the complete chain from originator to transferor, from transferor to trust, from trust back to transferor, and from transferor back to plaintiff, as applicable.
