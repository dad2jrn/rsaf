# American Express Playbook

**Status:** Research stub — EDGAR research required before use  
**Originating entity:** American Express Centurion Bank or American Express Bank, FSB (research required to confirm originating entity and program structure; charge-card vs. credit-card distinction may apply)

## Purpose

This playbook, when complete, will document RSAF's research on American Express's credit-card receivable structure. It is a research framework, not an advocacy file. It does not presume any outcome about any specific receivable.

Note: American Express issues both charge cards (which require payment in full each month) and revolving credit cards. The applicable securitization structure, transfer mechanics, and governing agreements may differ depending on which product the account was. Verify the product type and originating entity before applying this playbook to any case.

A playbook is complete only when the following have been documented from verified public sources: the relevant entities; the program structure; transfer mechanics; servicing and collection authority; trust or purchaser structure; default, removal, repurchase, and reassignment mechanics; governing-law provisions; and the account-level evidence a plaintiff would need to establish its enforcement theory for an account originating from this bank.

## Required Public Documents to Locate

- Corporate 10-K (American Express Company).
- Trust 10-K (annual report filed by the issuing trust, if applicable).
- Prospectuses and prospectus supplements.
- Receivables Purchase Agreement.
- Transfer and Servicing Agreement or Pooling and Servicing Agreement.
- Trust Agreement.
- Indenture.
- UCC financing statements.
- Cardmember agreement applicable to the account at issue.
- Distribution or servicing reports.

## Transfer Path (Generic Template — Pending Research)

The following represents the general structure of a credit-card securitization. American Express's actual structure must be confirmed from EDGAR before this diagram is applied to any case. The charge-card structure may differ materially from the revolving-credit structure.

```text
Originating Bank
        ↓
Transferor / Depositor / Bankruptcy-Remote Entity
        ↓
Issuing Trust
        ↓
Default / Removal / Reassignment mechanism (if applicable)
        ↓
Plaintiff, if receivable was reacquired
```

Whether the receivable was ever transferred, whether it was reacquired, and whether the plaintiff is the originating bank or a subsequent purchaser are questions of fact that must be determined from the governing agreements and account-level evidence in each case.

## Account-Level Proof (Generalized — Pending Program Research)

- Executed assignment or bill of sale identifying the defendant's account.
- Account schedule tied to an executed transfer instrument.
- Removal notice or removal schedule (if reacquisition theory).
- Executed reassignment instrument identifying this receivable (if reacquisition theory).
- Evidence of retention (if plaintiff claims the receivable was never transferred).
- Authentication and foundation for each document.
