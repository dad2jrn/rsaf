# Chase Playbook

**Status:** Research stub — EDGAR research required before use  
**Originating entity:** JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A. (research required to confirm current securitization structure and participant entities)

## Purpose

This playbook, when complete, will document RSAF's research on Chase's credit-card receivable structure. It is a research framework, not an advocacy file. It does not presume any outcome about any specific receivable.

A playbook is complete only when the following have been documented from verified public sources: the relevant entities; the program structure; transfer mechanics; servicing and collection authority; trust or purchaser structure; default, removal, repurchase, and reassignment mechanics; governing-law provisions; and the account-level evidence a plaintiff would need to establish its enforcement theory for an account originating from this bank.

Until that research is complete, do not draw conclusions about Chase's securitization program, transfer path, or documentation practices. Use the generic RSAF framework instead.

## Research Required

Before this playbook can be used, conduct EDGAR research to identify and verify:

- The issuing trust name and CIK number.
- The depositor or transferor entity and its relationship to the originating bank.
- The governing agreements: Receivables Purchase Agreement, Transfer and Servicing Agreement (or Pooling and Servicing Agreement), Trust Agreement, and Indenture.
- The servicer and the servicer's collection authority under the governing agreements.
- The trust structure (master trust, issuance trust, or both) and any distinctions between series.
- The eligibility criteria for receivables transferred under the program.
- The removal and reassignment provisions specifying conditions, procedures, and required documentation.
- The governing-law provisions for each agreement.
- Whether the bank has modified, wound down, or changed any aspect of the program since the account at issue was originated.

## Required Public Documents to Locate

- Corporate 10-K (JPMorgan Chase & Co. or JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A.).
- Trust 10-K (annual report filed by the issuing trust with the SEC).
- Prospectuses and prospectus supplements for individual series.
- Receivables Purchase Agreement.
- Transfer and Servicing Agreement or Pooling and Servicing Agreement.
- Trust Agreement.
- Indenture.
- UCC financing statements (search Delaware Secretary of State and any relevant state).
- Cardmember agreement applicable to the account at issue.
- Distribution or servicing reports (Form 10-D or ABS-15G).

## Playbook Structure When Complete

A complete playbook will document:

- **Entities:** Full name, jurisdiction, and role of each participant (originating bank, depositor, trust, servicer, indenture trustee, owner trustee, and any others).
- **Program structure:** Trust type; series structure; master trust vs. issuance trust distinctions.
- **Transfer mechanics:** How receivables are transferred at each step; what documents each step generates; what schedules or account-level records are required.
- **Servicing and collection authority:** What the governing agreement grants the servicer authority to do, including whether the servicer may sue in its own name.
- **Default, removal, repurchase, and reassignment mechanics:** What events trigger removal; what the removal procedure requires; what documentation is generated; where removed receivables go.
- **Governing-law provisions:** Which law governs each agreement; which law governs the trust; which law applies to account-level disputes.
- **Public source documents:** EDGAR links or identifiers for each key document.
- **Unresolved factual questions:** What cannot yet be determined from available public documents.
- **Case-specific evidence required:** What an analysis of a specific case would need to produce beyond the program documents.

## Account-Level Proof (Generalized — Pending Program Research)

The following categories of account-level evidence are typically relevant in cases involving securitized credit-card receivables. Specific requirements will depend on the governing agreements once researched.

- Executed assignment or bill of sale identifying the defendant's account.
- Account schedule tied to an executed transfer instrument.
- Removal notice or removal schedule (if reacquisition theory).
- Executed reassignment instrument identifying this receivable (if reacquisition theory).
- Evidence of retention (if plaintiff claims the receivable was never transferred).
- Authentication and foundation for each document.
